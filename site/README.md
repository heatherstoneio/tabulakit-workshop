# TabulaKit

## You took the leap. Here's what comes next.

You signed up for a workshop, and you might not be entirely sure what you're getting into. That's okay — that's exactly why you're here.

Here's the short version: **you're going to build a website that organizes information for your business.** Not a marketing site or an online store — a place where you can put all the things that live in your head, in scattered documents, in email chains, and in sticky notes, and turn them into something clear, organized, and accessible from anywhere.

The best part? You won't be writing code. You'll be talking to an AI assistant that understands your project and makes changes for you. You describe what you want in plain English, and it builds it.

## What could you build?

The system is flexible, but here are some real examples of what people use it for:

- **Run your whole business** — organize operations, track projects, document processes, and keep everything in one place instead of scattered across a dozen tools
- **Manage a complex project** — whether it's a product launch, a construction project, or navigating a complicated bureaucracy, keep every detail, timeline, and decision organized
- **Teach a course or workshop** — build a site with your syllabus, session materials, pre-work checklists, and resources that students can access from anywhere
- **Prepare for investors** — organize your business plan, financials, market research, and supporting documents into a polished, structured presentation
- **Plan an event or exercise** — track personnel, timelines, communications, risks, and logistics all in one place
- **Anything that needs structure** — a family vacation with lots of moving parts, a community organization's operations, a research project — if it would benefit from getting organized, this can help

You can create as many of these sites as you want, for free. But for the workshop, **pick one thing** — one area of your business or life that would be much better off if you simply got to a place of organization and clarity.

See [Case Studies](case-studies.md) for real-world examples of how this system is already being used.

## Before the Workshop

There are a few technical setup steps to complete before workshop day. They take about 10 minutes. If you get through them in advance, great — it means more time at the workshop working on things that matter to your business. If you get stuck, just set it aside. You'll get hands-on guidance at the workshop and you won't be behind.

### 1. Create a GitHub Account

Go to [github.com/signup](https://github.com/signup) and create a free account. GitHub is the service that stores your site and keeps track of every change you make — think of it like a smart filing cabinet in the cloud that you own.

### 2. Install VS Code

Download and install [Visual Studio Code](https://code.visualstudio.com/). This is the application where you'll do your work. Think of it as a workspace — you'll type, talk to the AI assistant, and see your changes here.

**Windows users:** You'll also need one extra step. Open PowerShell (search for it in your Start menu, right-click, "Run as Administrator") and paste this:
```
wsl --install
```
Restart your computer when it asks. Then open VS Code and install the **WSL** extension (click the square icon on the left sidebar, search "WSL", install it).

### 3. Install the Claude Code Extension

In VS Code, click the Extensions icon (the square on the left sidebar), search for **Claude Code**, and install it. You'll need a Claude account — sign up at [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) if you don't have one.

### 4. Verify It Works

Open VS Code, find Claude Code in the bottom panel, and try typing "hello". If the AI responds, you're all set.

### 5. Think About What to Build

This is the most important prep you can do. Before the workshop, think about:

- **What's the one thing** in your business (or life) that would benefit most from getting organized?
- **What materials do you already have?** Existing documents, spreadsheets, notes, emails — anything relevant. Consider having these accessible on your computer during the workshop so the AI can help you work with them.
- **Who needs access?** Just you? Your team? Clients? Students? This affects which setup options you'll choose.

You don't need to have all the answers. Just come with a direction in mind and whatever existing materials you have. The AI assistant will help you structure everything from there.

## At the Workshop

On the day, we'll walk through the [Getting Started](getting-started.md) guide together. You'll paste a prompt into the AI assistant and it will set up your entire site — creating your space on GitHub, configuring everything, and getting you to a working site. From there, you'll start adding your own content and making it yours.

By the end of the workshop, you'll have a live, working site that you can continue building on your own.
