# Planning Process

The Military Decision-Making Process (MDMP) adapted for any organization. A structured approach to planning that produces clear, actionable operations orders.

## Why Structured Planning Matters

Unstructured planning tends to jump to solutions. Structured planning forces you to understand the problem first, consider multiple approaches, and communicate the plan in a way others can execute independently.

## The Planning Steps

### 1. Receive the Mission

**What you do:** Understand the task and its context.

- What are we being asked to do?
- Why? (Commander's intent — what does success look like?)
- What constraints exist? (Time, resources, authorities)
- What's already been decided?

### 2. Mission Analysis

**What you do:** Break the mission apart to understand what's really required.

- What are the specified tasks? (Explicitly stated)
- What are the implied tasks? (Not stated but necessary)
- What are the constraints? (Must do / must not do)
- What assumptions are we making?
- What's the timeline?

**Output:** Restated mission in your own words. If you can't restate it clearly, you don't understand it.

### 3. Course of Action Development

**What you do:** Develop 2-3 ways to accomplish the mission.

For each option, answer:
- How does this accomplish the mission?
- What resources does it require?
- What are the risks?
- What's the timeline?

**Don't fall in love with your first idea.** The point of multiple courses of action is to avoid confirmation bias.

### 4. Course of Action Analysis

**What you do:** War-game each option against likely scenarios.

- What could go wrong?
- What does the enemy/competition/environment do?
- Where are the decision points?
- What triggers a change in plan?

### 5. Course of Action Comparison

**What you do:** Pick the best option based on your criteria.

| Criteria | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| Risk | | | |
| Resources | | | |
| Timeline | | | |
| Achieves intent | | | |

### 6. Orders Production

**What you do:** Produce the OPORD.

See [Orders & Communication](orders-communication.md) for the OPORD format.

## Common Planning Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Jumping to solutions | Force yourself through mission analysis first |
| Only one course of action | Require at least two options |
| Planning in isolation | Include the people who will execute |
| Perfect plan, no time to execute | Time-box planning — a good plan now beats a perfect plan too late |
| Ignoring assumptions | List them explicitly so you know when they break |
