# Operating Rhythm

The cadences and routines that keep an organization running without everything requiring the leader's direct attention.

## Why Rhythm Matters

Without rhythm, organizations operate reactively — every issue is an interrupt, every decision waits for the leader, and nothing happens until someone asks about it. An operating rhythm creates **predictable touchpoints** so that information flows, decisions get made, and work stays on track automatically.

## Suggested Cadences

### Daily: Standup

**Purpose:** Synchronize the team on today's priorities and blockers.

**Format:** Each person answers:
1. What am I working on today?
2. What's blocking me?
3. What does the team need to know?

**Rules:**
- Time-boxed (15 minutes max)
- Status updates, not problem-solving
- Blockers get noted for follow-up, not resolved in the meeting

### Weekly: Domain Sync

**Purpose:** Cross-functional coordination across the seven domains (S1-S7).

**Format:** Quick pass through each domain:
- S1 Personnel — team status, capacity
- S2 Intelligence — new information, market changes
- S3 Operations — current priorities, progress
- S4 Logistics — resource status, upcoming needs
- S5 Plans — strategic alignment, upcoming initiatives
- S6 Signal — communication issues, tool updates
- S7 Info Ops — external messaging, stakeholder updates

**At small scale:** One person does a mental pass through all seven. The value is in the checklist, not in having seven different people.

### Weekly: Leadership Update

**Purpose:** Strategic alignment and priority decisions.

**Format:**
1. Progress against top priorities
2. Decisions needed
3. Risks and issues
4. Priorities for next week

### Monthly: Planning Cycle

**Purpose:** Look ahead, adjust course, plan upcoming initiatives.

**Format:**
1. Review current operations — on track or not?
2. Review strategic objectives — still relevant?
3. Plan upcoming work — what's next, who owns it?
4. Resource allocation — do we need to shift?

### After Each Operation: AAR

**Purpose:** Learn from what just happened.

See [Execution Framework](execution-framework.md) for AAR format.

## Building Your Rhythm

Start simple and add cadences as needed:

1. **Start with a daily standup** — solves the "what's happening?" problem
2. **Add a weekly sync** — solves the "are we coordinated?" problem
3. **Add a monthly planning cycle** — solves the "where are we going?" problem
4. **Add AARs** — solves the "are we learning?" problem

**Don't add cadences you won't maintain.** A skipped meeting is worse than no meeting — it signals that the rhythm doesn't matter.

## Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Meeting to discuss what to discuss | No agenda, no decisions | Require a written agenda or cancel |
| Status meeting with 20 people | Wrong audience, wasted time | Only include decision-makers and contributors |
| Weekly meeting that's always cancelled | Signals it's not important | Cancel the recurring or make it matter |
| No written record | Decisions are forgotten | Capture decisions and action items |
